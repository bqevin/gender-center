Africa Centre for Gender, Social Research and Impact Assessment

The Africa Centre for Gender, Social Research and Impact Assessment is a company limited by liability and registered in Kenya under the Companies Act (Cap. 486) in 2011.

The centre is a consultancy company with the objectives of;

  • Working with research and development organizations to integrate gender in their programmes to achieve both program and project objectives and gender equality outcomes
  • Building the capacity of individuals and organizations to understand gender and implement gender focused programs
  • Supporting organizations in developing their gender strategies and implementation plans
  • Conducting mid and end term evaluation to analyse the social and economic impacts of projects and programs

 

Researching Gender and Agriculture: A Learning Program for Social Scientists

 

This course is borne out of the recognition of the important role gender plays in implementation of agricultural, food and nutrition security projects, programs and policies. Participants will develop a deep understanding of key gender and intra-household analytical concepts and methods that can facilitate the design, collection, analysis and interpretation of gender-sensitive data with potential to provide insights into larger processes of social and livelihood change. The course will be a practical, hands-on oriented course and sessions will innovatively link theory to practice, facilitate sharing of experiences and incorporate individual and collective learning.

WHERE: Nairobi, Kenya

WHEN: Module 1 (6-10 June and 21-25 November 2016: Module 2 (28 Nobember-2 December)

WHO: Social Scientists working in Agriculture
